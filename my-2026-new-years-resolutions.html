My New Year's Resolutions 2026 By Fred Fraser



How will 2026 be a better year for me?



I keep an old school paper wall calendar downstairs in my kitchen. Maybe I like it because it reminds me of a different era. A time when things were manual, things were slow and deliberate. A time when this is how we did things. A time when we scribbled directions on a napkin with a pencil and that was how we drove places. Our Google or AI was going to the local library and getting books, then actually reading and studying and memorizing things.


Back to the calendar.


It seems I just keep ripping pages and pages more and more often. The past year has flown by like no other. Each year feels faster than the next. I used to measure my time in terms of days or months. Even years. Now it's decades and that is scary. As I see more life behind me in the rear view mirror than in front of me, I am careful with my resolutions for the new year. Sure, lose weight, go to the gym, those are all good and noble. The problem is many resolutions don't go beyond February.


Here is my list. Let's hope I can keep these throughout the whole year.


Lose weight


I've been on a diet for well over a year now and I have lost weight. I aim to lose more but I have plateaued. With Christmas I feel I have gained a few (I don't own a scale). Though my clothes fit me the same. I run and work out 6 days a week and will continue to do so.


Work on my business


My company is new. I started it in July. As I write this, it is 5 months old. I have had a few clients and 5-star Google reviews. I am told I am ahead of the curve. Many new businesses have no clients and no reviews. So I am grateful for what I have achieved and will work hard to be present, show up, commit to good SEO practices and help small businesses get found online once and for all.
