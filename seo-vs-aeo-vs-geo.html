Quick Definitions

  • SEO (Search Engine Optimization): optimizing a website to rank in search engines like Google
  • AEO (Answer Engine Optimization): structuring content so AI systems can extract direct answers
  • GEO (Generative Engine Optimization): optimizing content so generative AI tools cite your website
Comparison of SEO, AEO, and GEO
Optimization Focus Goal
SEO Search engines Rank pages in search results
AEO Answer engines / AI-powered search Provide direct answers to user queries
GEO Generative AI systems Be cited in AI-generated responses and build authority




What is SEO?


SEO, or search engine optimization, is the practice of improving a website to increase its visibility in organic (non-paid) search engine results, such as on Google. By optimizing content, technical structure, and building authority, SEO helps websites rank higher, driving more targeted traffic and improving user experience.

Key components of SEO include:

  • On-Page SEO: Optimizing individual pages with relevant keywords, content, and meta tags.
  • Off-Page SEO: Building credibility and authority through backlinks from other websites.
  • Technical SEO: Enhancing website performance, such as speed, crawlability, and mobile-friendliness, to help engines index the site.


What is AEO?


Answer Engine Optimization (AEO) is the process of structuring website content to be directly cited, summarized, or spoken by AI-powered search tools (like ChatGPT, Gemini, Perplexity, and Google AI Overviews). Unlike traditional SEO, which focuses on ranking links, AEO optimizes for concise, direct, and authoritative answers to user queries, including voice search.

Key components of AEO include:

  • Focus on Q&A: Content is structured to answer specific "who, what, why, how" questions directly.
  • Structured Data: Utilizing schema markup to help AI understand and process content.
  • High-Quality Content: Emphasizing accuracy, E-E-A-T (Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trustworthiness), and clear, concise, and structured information.
  • Voice Search Readiness: Ensuring content is easily read by virtual assistants such as Alexa or Siri.
  • Zero-Click Results: The goal is to appear in the immediate answer box, rather than just in the list of links below it.


What is GEO?


Generative Engine Optimization (GEO) is the next evolution of SEO designed for the AI age, focusing on optimizing content to get cited by AI-powered search tools like ChatGPT, Gemini, and Google's AI Overview. Unlike traditional SEO that aims for top links, GEO maximizes visibility within AI-generated responses to build brand trust, credibility, and authority.

Key components of GEO include:

  • Purpose: To make content discoverable and authoritative enough to be cited by LLMs (Large Language Models).
  • Tactics: Similar to SEO, it requires creating high-quality, relevant content, but with added focus on answering specific user intent and, crucially, technical accessibility for AI crawlers.
  • Metrics: Moves beyond clicks to track "share of voice" or "share of model" (brand mentions) across platforms like Perplexity, Gemini, and Claude.
  • Connection to SEO: Effective GEO often stems from strong, foundational SEO, making them complementary strategies.

How AI Finds Your Website


AI systems perform best when information is stated clearly and directly. In other words, AI loves explicit language. Who, what, when, why, how. You're a plumber? What's your name? Where's your business located? Who do you serve? What areas do you serve? Who are you? Why do you do what you do? Tell me about you.

AI loves graphs, tables, bulleted items, all kinds of logical representations.

AI systems prefer pages that have:

  • Clear headings
  • Concise explanations
  • Lists and bullet points
  • Structured data / schema
  • Fast page load times
  • Authoritative expertise

How to Optimize for AI Search


So what is the answer? How do you optimize your website for AI search?

  1. Use clear question-style headings
  2. Provide concise answers. Remember AI loves clarity.
  3. Add schema markup. This is not visible to human viewers.
  4. Include authoritative expertise. You want to be that one, or in the two or three, that AI cites.
  5. Structure content for skimming.
  6. Build trusted backlinks. Remember no spammy backlinks, or links to nowhere.
