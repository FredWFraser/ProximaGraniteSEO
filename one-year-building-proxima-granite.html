One Year Building Proxima Granite SEO: What I Learned by Fred Fraser

“Build it and they will come”. This famous phrase is from the classic 1989 film “Field of Dreams”. An Iowa farmer hears a voice telling him “build it and he will come” meaning if he built a baseball diamond in his cornfield, Shoeless Joe Jackson will come. The phrase has evolved from “he” to “they” over time.

I thought of that phrase as I created my own business. Build it and they will come. The truth is, that phrase couldn't be further from the truth.

The Beginning

I started my journey taking a course in web design. I then proceeded to take another course, in how to start a home-based SEO business. I had no idea what SEO was. SEO stands for Search Engine Optimization and I learned all about keywords, backlinks, meta descriptions and such, all new terms to me. I dove into the mysterious, new exciting world of SEO and took yet another course in SEO itself, immersing myself in all the basics.

I thought this would be a great idea for a company. After losing my corporate job in engineering, I was growing tired of working life. Performance reviews, management pressure, absolutely constant meetings after meetings and more meetings. Half hour scheduled meetings became one and a half hours long. With glazed over eyes and wanting to be anywhere else, what choice did I have? I had to put up with remote work with the advent of Covid. Again, constant Teams calls. Online team building games. Reviews that really weren't constructive yet they came up so much.

I lost my job and sent countless applications, had so many interviews and came up with nothing. So, I decided I must start a company. A home-based SEO business was a great idea. I could work remotely, cater to anyone in the country (or world really), could work from anywhere, had an extremely low overhead. All I needed to do was build a website, get a company name, and I was off to fame and fortune.

I learned the hard way it doesn't work that way.

My web design course really helped me in building my own website from scratch. I used my HTML, CSS and Javascript knowledge, deployed to GitHub and had myself a fast, responsive website of my own. I used Google Search Console to track my progress. Within two weeks I started to rank. I was targeting small New Hampshire businesses, plumbers, roofers, barbershops and such. I was posting on social media twice a week. Within months I got my first paying clients. I got Google five-star reviews that I could tout on my website. I joined the local chamber of commerce and learned to pass out cards, shake hands, and talk before a group about my growing business.

I had one potential client who seemed willing to pay me, but wouldn't share his social media, or images. I had another few prospects. One I went back and forth with for weeks and weeks on end. I revised my proposal three times. The answer was no. Not because of my ability, but they were skittish about the market for their business and didn't want to spend the money on technical SEO fixes. Lesson learned, proposal does not equal “yes”.

Proxima Pulse: My Own SEO Software Product

I build a software product from scratch. “Proxima Pulse” is my proprietary product built from Python. It accepts not only a URL but a FILE as well (commercial products don't process files). I wanted to understand SEO better and came up with a commercial product that provides 25 key insights within seconds. I even had it connected to AI as well, but decoupled it for now due to performance issues. I even developed my own testing framework to run regression tests on the product. I built it using Java, Selenium, and TestNG. I even recorded and produced my own demo video and posted in on YouTube. So not only was I a website developer, product developer, business owner, but now I became the “face” of my business, and spokesman.

Here Comes AI

Then along came AI. Not only do websites have to be interesting to humans, cater to Google but now, they have be loved by AI was well. Not only AI in general, but the many flavors and personalities of the AI chatbots. Now, the game is not only about getting seen in the search engine result page, ranking “number one”, but websites have to be cited by AI, or be “the answer” (hence the term AEO, or Answer Engine Optimization). Though SEO has not gone away, nor do I believe it ever will, foundational SEO is still critical in the search infrastructure. AI is searching for websites, and signals such as Google Business Profile, reviews, all the things that make for a good company. AI utilizes structured data, or schema markup to understand content. AI wants high quality content. AI wants clear headings, concise explanations, schema, fast page load times, and authoritative expertise. SEO is more important than ever in getting discovered not just in Google but for AI discovery as well. The irony is Google is starting to reward websites that publish original, human content, as opposed to AI-generated content. The standard among web developers was to have AI simply generate pages for blogs. The rule of thumb was it didn't matter who wrote it, just that it was grammatically correct and had the correct word count. Now, human written content is increasingly being rewarded. I write all my insights myself, from my own mind and heart, my own experiences. I use my humor, thoughtfulness, not to be rewarded by Google, but so readers can understand more about me and my work.

In Conclusion

I have refined my message. A year ago it was “I do SEO”. Today, it is “I help businesses with technical SEO from an engineering and QA perspective, backed by tools I built myself”. I have spent 365 days testing an idea, refining my position, and discovering where my strengths truly lie.

In one year I started a real business, built a website from scratch and got it online, learned technical SEO, built a software product to perform an SEO audit, earned paying clients, wrote technical articles about my very own experience, refined my positioning, and just kept going. I am now in my second year and will just keep going, keep learning and strive to serve clients with solid, technical SEO.
